What is the NIS2 Directive?

The NIS2 Directive (Network and Information Security Directive 2) is the EU's updated legislation on cybersecurity, replacing the original NIS Directive. It came into effect in January 2023 and member states have until October 2024 to transpose it into national law.

Broader Scope

NIS2 expands coverage to more sectors and includes medium and large entities across critical infrastructure.

Stricter Requirements

Enhanced security measures, incident reporting obligations, and supply chain security requirements.

Management Accountability

Executive management is now personally liable for ensuring compliance with cybersecurity measures.

Key Compliance Requirements

  • Risk Management: Implement comprehensive risk management measures for network and information systems
  • Incident Reporting: Report significant incidents within 24 hours (early warning) and full reports within 72 hours
  • Business Continuity: Develop and test business continuity and disaster recovery plans
  • Supply Chain Security: Assess and manage cybersecurity risks in supplier relationships
  • Encryption & Access Control: Implement appropriate encryption and multi-factor authentication
  • Security Training: Provide regular cybersecurity awareness training to employees
  • Vulnerability Management: Establish processes to handle and disclose vulnerabilities
